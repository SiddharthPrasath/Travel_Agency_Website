Tips For Travel

Choose Locations

While it is important to plan what place you will visiting, make sure you don't over do it by panning each and every single second of your trip.

Book Hotels

Decide where you will be sleeping accordiing to your budget and make sure you book rooms before coming as rooms are harder to find if you don't prebook.

Enjoy Yourself

Once you have booked the plane or train tickets all you need to is focus on enjoying yourself. Make sure you gulp take the beauty of the city.

places to visit!

These are some of the most popular places in Mumbai city that any tourist must visit.

Apollo Bandar, Colaba
Gateway of India

Located next to the iconic Taj Mahal Palace and facing the vast Arabian Sea, the Gateway of India leaves you spellbound in every way possible..

Gorai, Borivali West
Global Vipassana Pagoda

Its traditional Burmese design is an expression of gratitude towards the country of Myanmar for preserving the practice of Vipassana.

Borivali East, Mumbai
Kanheri Caves

Accessible via rock-cut steps. The cave complex comprises one hundred and nine caves.Each cave has a stone plinth that functioned as a bed.

Gharapuri, Mumbai
Elephanta Caves

Take a tour to this UNESCO World Heritage Site in Mumbai and get wonderstruck by the massive rock-cut caves that date back to several centuries.

Borivali East, Mumbai
Sanjay Gandhi National Park

Sprawling over an area of 104 sq. km. of land, this is one of the most visited national parks in Asia, this park can surely be a family entertainer. You can watch the big cats of the park.

Amrut Nagar, Ghatkopar
Red Carpet Wax Museum

It exhibits lifelike wax figures of prominent icons from various fields including science, politics, sports and world cinema. So, take selfies with your favorite icon.

Dargah Road, Mumbai
Haji Ali Dargah

This grand edifice is made of white marbles and exemplifies the Indo-Islamic style of architecture. A walk through the causeway that connects the Dargah to the Lala Rajput Rai Marg is the main attraction of this place.

CST Area, Mumbai
Chhatrapathi Shivaji Terminus

Chatrapathi Shivaji Terminus is another UNESCO World Heritage Site situated right in the heart of Mumbai. The magnificent CST is the most photographed building in India after Taj Mahal!
